## Color Application Guide

### How to Apply Colors

**Gradients:**
- Center → Edge: Most common (burst effects)
- Leading → Trailing: Motion effects (trails)
- Core → Outer: Layered effects (flames)

**Contrast:**
- Always maintain high saturation
- Use complementary colors sparingly
- Black outlines must stand out against all colors

**Glow Effects:**
- Add bright core color
- Transition to darker outer
- Subtle halo glow outside outline

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Soft edges** - Keep outlines CRISP and BOLD
2. **Low saturation** - Colors should be VIBRANT
3. **No outline** - Black outline is MANDATORY
4. **Too realistic** - Keep it CEL-SHADED and stylized
5. **Multiple particles** - ONE particle per texture
6. **White background** - Must be TRANSPARENT
7. **Semi-transparent halo** - Pure alpha only
8. **Too small** - Use 70-90% of canvas space
9. **Too complex** - Readability at 50% scale matters
10. **Wrong dimensions** - Must be exactly 512x512px

---

## Quality Standards

**GOOD Examples:**
- Thick bold black outlines (3-5px)
- High contrast, vibrant colors
- Clean crisp edges
- Centered composition
- Single focused particle
- Subtle African pattern integration
- Arcade game energy

**BAD Examples:**
- Thin or no outlines
- Muted pastel colors
- Blurry soft edges
- Off-center composition
- Multiple separate particles
- Overly detailed patterns obscuring form
- Realistic photo style

# Audio Credits

_Spar card game - sound effects (Build Ticket 10)._

## Summary

Every sound effect shipped in the game is **original audio, synthesized
programmatically** by [`scripts/generate_sfx.py`](scripts/generate_sfx.py) from
math primitives (sine / sawtooth / square / noise, plus envelopes and filters).
Nothing is sampled, recorded, or copied from any third-party sound library.

- **License:** Original work, owned by the project.
- **Commercial use:** Safe. Because we generate and own these bytes outright,
  there are no third-party rights to clear.
- **Format:** Mono, 44100 Hz, 16-bit PCM WAV (matches what the frontend loads in
  `frontend/src/services/audioManager.ts`).
- **Reproducible:** The generator seeds its RNG per clip, so re-running produces
  byte-identical assets. See [`AUDIO_MANIFEST.json`](AUDIO_MANIFEST.json) for the
  per-key file map and durations.

## Soundalike disclosure (honest description)

Two clips are deliberate **soundalikes** - they EVOKE well-known arcade-announcer
moments but are NOT copies and contain no sampled material:

- **Fire streak "on fire!" sting** (`sound:fire_streak`) - evokes the general
  "hype announcer + flames" energy associated with games like NBA Jam / Mortal
  Kombat. It is assembled entirely from synthesis: a high-passed noise crackle
  (flames), a rising pitch sweep (the announcer's lift), a bright chord stab
  (the payoff), and a generic formant "exclamation" blip. There are no words, no
  sampled recording, and no attempt to match the specific pitch contour, phrase,
  or vocal timbre of any trademarked recording.

- **"Toasty" easter-egg byte** (`toasty.wav`) - evokes the surprise arcade shout
  associated with Mortal Kombat. It is a quick, high, rising vocal-ish formant
  blip (a generic rising vowel shape) with a tiny noise onset and a bright tail
  ping. Again: no words, no sample, no timbre/pitch match of the trademarked
  clip.

Basis for asserting these are not disguised rips: both are produced purely by
algorithmic synthesis in `scripts/generate_sfx.py` from oscillators, noise,
envelopes and formant-shaped filters. No audio file was downloaded, decoded,
time-stretched, pitch-shifted, or otherwise transformed from a source recording.
The "toasty" clip is currently an unwired asset (not loaded by the game); its
intended trigger is documented in `AUDIO_MANIFEST.json`.
